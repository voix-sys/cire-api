## Executive summary (10 lines)
1. Strongest synergy signal is for **adapalene + benzoyl peroxide** (multiple RCTs + 2024 guideline support).
2. Escalation to **triple acne combination** (clindamycin+BPO+adapalene) shows additional efficacy in moderate–severe acne.
3. For melasma, high-quality evidence favors **fluocinolone + hydroquinone + tretinoin** as an effective short-course combo.
4. Antioxidant photoprotection is one of the clearest non-acne synergies: **vitamin C + vitamin E + ferulic acid**.
5. **Niacinamide** repeatedly appears as a tolerance/barrier enhancer and useful adjunct in sebum and dyschromia goals.
6. Rosacea evidence supports phenotype-guided use of **azelaic acid/metronidazole/ivermectin** rather than indiscriminate stacking.
7. Acid combinations (AHA+BHA; BHA with retinoid regimens) can improve outcomes but carry higher irritation/conflict risk.
8. Xerosis data supports pairing keratolytics (urea/lactic or salicylic) with moisturization for smoother barrier recovery.
9. Across conditions, combination benefit depends on **vehicle, concentration, and sequencing** more than ingredient names alone.
10. Practical formulation rule: prioritize combinations with RCT/guideline backing; treat multi-acid/retinoid stacks as high-risk unless titrated.
